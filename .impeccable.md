## Design Context

### Users
CarbonTrack serves individuals and small businesses that need to log travel, energy, food, and shopping activity quickly, then inspect carbon trends and reduction goals without analytical clutter.

### Brand Personality
Practical, calm, evidence-led. The interface should feel like a focused operations dashboard for environmental accounting rather than a marketing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained light data-workbench look with firm grid structure, compact metric panels, green and amber accents, high-contrast text, and dense but readable tables. Avoid oversized hero sections, decorative blobs, and generic gradient-heavy visuals.

### Design Principles
- Show usable carbon data on the first screen.
- Keep workflows close to the entities: users, activities, factors, goals, audit logs.
- Prefer scan-friendly controls and direct manipulation over explanatory copy.
- Use charts and progress components only where they answer a concrete question.
- Maintain mobile readability without hiding core actions.
